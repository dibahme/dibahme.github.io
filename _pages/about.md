I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at EPFL, advised by Martin Jaggi.

My research focuses on developing more capable and efficient large foundation models. I am interested in addressing these challenges from both algorithmic and modeling perspectives, spanning core computational efficiency to high-level reasoning.

Currently, I focus on:

- Innovations in model architectures to reduce inference and training costs
- Data-centric and communication-efficient strategies for large-scale pretraining
- Methods for advancing the general reasoning abilities of large models

You can find my full background, publications, teaching, and service on the [CV page](/cv/).
